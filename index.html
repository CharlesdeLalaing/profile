Telling fun facts

Do you know why car tires are black? Well, it's actually quite a funny story. At first, they weren't black at all, they were white ! That is because the natural color of caoutchouc is white. Car manufactures quickly realized that the natural state of the caoutchouc wasn't solid enough and that the tires needed to be replaced often. They then decided to had pigment of black of carbon. Which gave a more robust tire and a black color to the tire. At first, it was only the border of the tire that was black, the inside stayed white (you might have seen that in old movies with vintage cars). They then came to the conclusion to make the all tire black with the black of carbon, this to give it the best longevity. BUT that's not it... Now, the hardness of the tire isn't coming from the black of carbon anymore, but from another substance I forgot the name of. The black of carbon is now only used to give that black color to your car's tires.
